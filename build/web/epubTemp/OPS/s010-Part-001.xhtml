The Flight Log

Kenneth Pipe

The next 51 pages have images of each Flying Log page-spread with some annotations where they may be helpful or interesting. Some of the pages have expanding pictures. 

The log starts in New Zealand with training at Ohakea and then the move to North Africa for the Western Desert Campaign, and finally to England.

The first date entry is 10 July 1940 with training on Vincents at Ohakea in New Zealand.

The training was completed on the 26 October 1940 and the next entries starting from 3 March 1941 are from 70 Squadron in Kabrit, Egypt.

He was posted to the U.K. on the 3 February 1942.

The next few months were in various Operational Training Units (OTU) in Wellingtons, and then conversion training for the Lancester started on 5 July 1943.

Flying in 115 Squadron commenced on the 9 August 1943. Seven missions were flown, interspersed with training and other operations until the plane went missing on 22 September 1953 on his 53rd active mission (Pages 81-82).

The remainder of the log entries are after the war and are an informal record of flights during his peace-time service in the RNZAF. Pages 85-90 highly some of his work as Air Movements Officer at Whenuapai Airport in the Changi (Singapore) airlifts from 1958 to 1961 for which he was awarded the MBE.
